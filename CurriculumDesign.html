Portfolio Sample: Engaging Lesson Plan

Engaging Lesson Plan with Slide Deck

Lesson Plan Title: Click, Clack, Moo AND Giggle, Giggle, Quack

Grade Level: Kindergarten
Subject: Reading and English Language Arts
Duration: 60 minutes

Lesson Overview:

This lesson reviews story elements and enhances student comprehension by comparing character experiences through interactive discussions, group activities, and a guided slide presentation.

  • Objective: By the end of the lesson, students will be able to identify literary elements as well as compare and contrast character experiences.
  • Standards Alignment: FLA.K.R.1.1, 3.2, 3.3

Key Components:

  1. Lesson Plan: Organized with clear objectives, materials, lesson sequence, and assessment details. Incorporates interactive group activities and discussion prompts for student engagement.
    View Lesson Plan (PDF)
  2. Slide Deck: Dynamic visuals to introduce and explain renewable energy concepts. Interactive features like embedded videos and discussion prompts.
    View Slide Deck (Google Slides)

Highlights from the Slide Deck:

  • Introduction Slide: Best instructional practices covering the objectives and focus for the lesson; includes essential questions.
  • Content Slides: Easy to follow and guided for clear teacher instruction. Is also visually engaging for students.
  • Interactive Features: Embedded discussion questions, class activity instructions, and smart board featured writing activity.
  • Wrap up Slide: Summarizes key expectations for writing to aid in student demonstration of knowledge.

Assessment:

  • Formative: Group participation and responses during the discussion and T-chart.
  • Summative: Submission of student opinion writing.

Feedback and Outcomes:

Over 85% of teachers who implemented the lessons at their school reported increased student understanding of comparing character experiences as well as identifying literary elements. The interactive and chunked elements in the slide deck received positive feedback from both students and educators for keeping the lesson engaging.
